Evan Mast and Mike Stroud form the Brooklyn electronic rock duo know as RATATAT. Ratatat debuted in 2003 with the release of the single "Seventeen Years" on Mast's and his brother Eric's (E*Rock) record label Audio Dregs in the US and Rex Records in the UK. Having signed for XL Recordings, Ratatat released their self-titled debut album. The album was written and recorded in Mast's Crown Heights, Brooklyn apartment on his laptop. Ratatat's live performances bear strong similarities to the performances of the psychedelic rock bands out of San Francisco in the 1960s, with energetic light shows, swirling colors projected onto a screen, and clips of movies thrown in. Evan Mast creates and edits the videos that are projected on the screen. Many of them are included on their YouTube account.
